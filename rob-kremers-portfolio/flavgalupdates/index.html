📱 Onboarding Redesign (Parent-Friendly, Transformation-Focused)

Overview

Goal: Parents understand the transformation BEFORE committing data. Educate on SOS method, build trust, explain bridge foods, show value.

"Gentle Progress, Not Forced Eating" "The SOS Approach – Used by Therapists Worldwide"

Body Copy:

We use the Sequential Oral Sensory (SOS) Approach—the same method occupational therapists and speech therapists have used for 30+ years. Here's how it works: Instead of pressure or bribes, we work WITH your child's nervous system. 👁️ LOOK — Getting used to seeing the food 🙌 TOUCH — Exploring textures with their hands 👃 SMELL — Experiencing the aroma 👅 LICK — Trying tiny tastes 😋 TASTE — Eating and enjoying Each phase takes 3-10 days. No rushing. Your child's nervous system needs time to process each new sensation. That's not slow—that's smart. Research shows children using the SOS Approach see measurable improvements in food acceptance within weeks to months—not years of battles.

Headline:

"The Clever Way to Expand Their Diet: Bridge Foods"

Body Copy:

Here's where the magic happens. Your child has foods they already like and eat comfortably. Chicken nuggets. Crackers. Pasta. Whatever they trust. A "bridge food" shares the same sensory qualities as those safe foods—but introduces something slightly new. Real Example: → If they like crunchy, salty crackers → Try crunchy, salty pretzels (similar texture + taste, but new) → Then gradually explore other crunchy snacks It's not random. It's strategic. You're meeting them where they are, then slowly expanding from there. How We Help: This app analyzes your child's favorite foods, identifies what they like about them (the textures, flavors, temperatures), and suggests bridge foods they're most likely to accept. Using AI, we personalize recommendations to YOUR child's unique sensory profile. This is how selective eaters become adventurous eaters. One bridge at a time.

🧠 Bridge Food LLM Integration (Qwen 3.5 Plus)

Why LLM?

Current algorithm = distance-based matching (generic). LLM = contextual understanding + parent-friendly explanations + learning loop.

Qwen 3.5 Plus Pricing

Input:  $0.11 per 1M tokens
Output: ~$0.66 per 1M tokens

100K users × 52 weeks × 2 recs/week = 10.4M calls/year
Total: ~$2,600/year ✅ (vs Claude: $62K/year ❌)

LLM Prompt (Production)

System Prompt: You are a Sensory Feeding Expert specializing in the SOS (Sequential Oral Sensory) Approach. Your role is to recommend "bridge foods" that match a child's sensory profile with high likelihood of acceptance. A bridge food: - Shares sensory qualities (texture, flavor, temperature) with foods the child already eats - Introduces ONE new sensory element at a time - Is age-appropriate and available - Matches the child's current risk tolerance You consider: 1. Safe foods they've already accepted (recent history) 2. Textures they avoid (aversions) 3. Flavors they're exploring 4. Temperature preferences 5. Color variety (dietary diversity) 6. Current planet/phase (progression pace) 7. Previous bridge food outcomes (learning) Output: Top 3-5 bridge foods with PARENT-FRIENDLY explanations (not clinical): - Food name + emoji - Why this works (specific, warm, conversational) - Sensory match (crunchy, sweet, soft, etc.) - Risk level (Low/Medium/High) - How to introduce it Example output format: [ { "name": "Cheese Quesadilla", "why": "Combines warm (like eggs/nuggets) + soft + cheesy (subtle salty flavor progression from bland nuggets)", "sensory_match": "Soft, warm, slightly crunchy edges, savory-adjacent", "risk": "LOW", "next_steps": "Start by letting her help make it, touch the tortilla before cooking, then taste a small corner" } ]

Implementation Details

  • API endpoint: POST /api/bridge-foods/recommend
  • Input: Child's sensory profile + safe foods + history
  • Cache: 24 hours (don't call LLM every page load)
  • Fallback: Revert to current algo if API fails
  • Learning: Track parent feedback (like/skip/reject) → refine future recs
