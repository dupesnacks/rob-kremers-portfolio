Engineered for Real-World Operations

Acceleration & Power

Three-motor architecture delivers 1,000+ horsepower equivalent and 1,200+ foot-pounds of torque. Accelerate loaded. Climb grades. Match or exceed diesel performance in real-world driving.

Durability & Reliability

Electric powertrain has dramatically fewer moving parts than diesel. No transmission, no engine oil degradation, no timing belts. Parts that can't fail don't require maintenance. Result: 1,000,000+ mile vehicle lifespan.

Charging Flexibility

MCS 3.2 charging available at Gigafactory Nevada. V4 Supercharger technology enables 1.2 MW charging. Charge overnight for short routes. Rapid charge during breaks for long-haul. Your choice.

Driver Experience

Instant torque response. Zero noise. Superior sight lines from centered seating position. Advanced climate control. Built-in aggregated dashcam system. Make driving safer, more comfortable, more efficient.

Autonomous Readiness

Hardware-ready for Tesla Full Self-Driving. Not available at launch, but the platform is prepared. When autonomy is ready, your fleet can upgrade via software.

Grade Performance

Multi-motor design provides superior grade climbing. Loaded ascents up to 6% grades maintain 65 mph performance. No engine lag. Consistent power delivery.

Environmental & Social Impact

Every Tesla Semi removes 6,000 tons of CO2 annually compared to diesel equivalent. Scale this across a fleet of 100 trucks: 600,000 tons. At 500 trucks: 3 million tons annually.

Beyond emissions, electric trucks reduce noise pollution, eliminate fuel spills, and improve air quality in communities where routes pass through.

Federal Incentives: Eligible for up to $40,000 in tax credits and various state rebates. Reduces effective purchase price significantly.

Charging Infrastructure

Gigafactory Nevada features dedicated Tesla Semi charging infrastructure. MCS 3.2 chargers provide rapid charging designed specifically for Class 8 trucks. Network expansion planned for 2025-2026 to support distributed fleet operations.

Charging Strategy for Fleets

  • Depot Charging (Overnight): Slower, cheaper overnight charging at your facility. 100% charge ready for next day.
  • Route Charging (Rapid): V4 Supercharger stops during driver breaks. 20 minutes to 80%.
  • Load Planning: Route optimization software suggests charge points based on cargo, distance, and energy consumption.
