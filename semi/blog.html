The Total Cost of Ownership Revolution in Heavy Trucking

For decades, diesel dominated trucking economics. Cheap fuel, established supply chains, and driver familiarity made gasoline combustion the default choice. But the equation has changed fundamentally.

Today, the math favors electric. Not by a small margin. By a historic one. A Tesla Semi operating at typical fleet utilization (200K+ miles annually) generates $250K+ in fuel savings alone over 5 years. Add maintenance, add federal incentives, and the financial case becomes overwhelming.

But here's what matters most: this isn't about environmentalism. It's about competitive advantage. The fleets that transition first will benefit from lower operating costs, reduced downtime, and infrastructure advantage. The laggards will pay the price.
