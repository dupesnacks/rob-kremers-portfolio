Actually

Stop letting your memberships go to waste.

The app that reminds families to use what they already paid for.

Executive Summary

Actually is a mobile app designed for families with young children who purchase memberships (zoos, museums, trampoline parks, community centers, etc.) but rarely use them. The app tracks memberships and subscriptions, calculates cost-per-visit, and sends timely reminders paired with contextual nudges to get families out the door. The core insight: parents don't want to cancel; they want permission and motivation to go. Actually makes that simple, social, and satisfying.

The Problem

Parents with young families sign up for memberships with the best intentions. "The kids will love the zoo!" "Science is important!" But life gets busy. Soccer practice, work deadlines, decision fatigue. Months pass. The membership renewal comes and goes—still unused. Guilt sets in.

Existing solutions are broken:

  • Subscription trackers (Rocket Money, Bobby): Help you cancel memberships, not use them
  • Gym apps: Remind you to go, but most families aren't paying for gyms—they're paying for experiences
  • Calendar apps: Require manual planning. No motivation. No guilt.
  • Nothing addresses the emotional core: Parents want to say yes to their kids, but need the nudge

The Insight

The real value isn't in canceling memberships. It's in actually using them—and making that feel easier, guilt-free, and even fun.

When a parent sees "You haven't been to OMSI in 4 months. Cost per visit: $60. Expires Dec 31," something shifts. Guilt activates, but so does urgency. A simple reminder that says "Saturday is perfect. Weather is good. Kids would love it" doesn't feel like nagging—it feels like permission.

Core Functionality

📋 Simple Entry

User adds membership: name, annual cost, category (zoo, museum, gym, etc.), expiration date. No verification required. Quick setup—under 2 minutes for 5 memberships.

📊 Dashboard at a Glance

Each membership shows: last visit date, cost per visit, days since last use, expiration countdown, "Mark Went Today" button. One screen, full visibility.

⏰ Smart Reminders

Triggers based on: last visit date (>30 days = notification), expiration countdown (<90 days = weekly reminder), weather/context (sunny day + outdoor membership = suggestion), day of week (Saturday = family time).

💰 Cost Per Visit Tracking

App calculates: "You've been 3 times. Cost per visit: $40. At current pace, you're losing $15/month by not going." Guilt + math = motivation.

🎉 Mark & Share

After a visit, user taps "Went Today!" Builds habit. Optional: "Share" button generates: "We finally used our zoo membership! Kids loved it 🦁" with cost savings highlighted.

📈 Weekly Summary

Visual dashboard: "You used $47 of memberships this week!" Calendar heatmap shows when family uses memberships. Trends emerge ("We always go on Saturdays").

App User Journey

Step 1: Signup & Onboarding (2 min)

Parent installs app. Quick onboarding: "Tell us about your memberships." User adds 3-5 memberships (name, cost, category, expiration). App immediately shows dashboard.

Step 2: First Dashboard View

Parent sees: "OMSI ($120/year, last visit 4 months ago, cost per visit: $60). Oregon Zoo ($180/year, last visit 2 months ago, cost per visit: $36). Trampoline Park ($200/year, last visit 1 week ago)."

Emotional reaction: "Wait, we haven't been to OMSI in that long? And we're spending $60 per visit now?"

Step 3: First Reminder (Day 3-7)

App sends: "You haven't been to OMSI in 4 months. Saturday is beautiful. Kids would love the new space exhibit. Let's go!"

This feels like a friend suggesting a fun outing, not a nag.

Step 4: Family Goes

Family decides to go. App is open, they tap "Going Today!" when they arrive (or later that day).

Step 5: Share & Celebrate

App shows: "Cost per visit is now $40 (down from $60)!" Option to share: "We finally used our OMSI membership! Kids loved it 🚀"

Step 6: Habit Loop

Each week, app shows cost savings. Parent gets dopamine hit from using membership. Friends see social post, ask "What app is that?"

Alert & Reminder Strategy

When Alerts Trigger:

Days Since Last Visit > 30: "You haven't been to [Membership] in [X] days. Cost per visit is now $[Y]. This week is perfect to go!"
Expiration Countdown < 90 days: "Your [Membership] expires in [X] days. You've gone [Y] times. Time to make it count?"
Weather-Based (Phase 2): "Sunny Saturday + you have a zoo membership unused for 6 weeks = Perfect timing. The kids would love it."
Day of Week Pattern: "Saturday is your favorite day to use memberships. You have [3 options]. Which sounds fun?"
High Cost Per Visit Alert: "Your [Membership] is now $55 per visit. At this pace, you'll lose $120 this year. One visit this month gets you back on track."

Frequency & Tone:

  • Max 2 notifications per week (not spammy, but enough to matter)
  • Never on school days (parents are overwhelmed)
  • Saturday mornings preferred (family decision-making time)
  • Tone: Friendly, not guilty ("Let's go have fun" not "You're wasting money")

Nudge Strategy: How We Get Families to Actually Go

Psychological Nudges (Subtle, Effective):

1. Visibility Nudge: Just seeing "Last visit: 4 months ago" makes guilt activate. No need for aggressive messaging.

2. Cost Per Visit Nudge: "$60 per visit" is scarier than "$120/year." Reframing cost makes impact visceral.

3. Expiration Countdown: "Expires Dec 31" creates urgency without pressure. "Better use it before it's gone."

4. Weekend Targeting: Reminders land Saturday morning when families are planning. NOT mid-week when stress is high.

5. Social Proof: "The Johnsons went to OMSI this weekend." (Optional: If friends are using the app.)

6. Permission Framing: "Saturday is perfect. Here's what's happening at OMSI." Gives parents permission + ideas. Not guilt, but guidance.

7. Progress Celebration: After a visit: "Cost per visit is now $45 (down from $60)!" Immediate positive reinforcement.

8. Friction Reduction: "Parking costs $5. Opens at 10 AM. Weather is 65°F." Remove decision-making friction.

Monetization Strategy

Freemium Model:

  • Free: Track unlimited memberships, reminders, cost-per-visit calculation, share basic stats
  • Premium ($2.99/month or $29.99/year):
    • Advanced analytics (trending, predictions)
    • Family leaderboards with friends
    • Smart recommendations (Phase 2: weather-based, event-based)
    • Calendar sync with Google Calendar/Outlook

Future Revenue (Phase 2+):

  • Affiliate: Partner with local attractions for special "Actually Users" discounts
  • B2B: Museums/zoos pay to be in the app, get engagement insights
  • Data: Anonymized membership trends sold to local tourism boards

Success Metrics

  • DAU (Daily Active Users): Target 40%+ of installed base opens app weekly
  • Visits Logged: Average user marks "went today" 2-3x per month
  • Share Rate: 20%+ of visits result in social share
  • Retention: Day 7 retention >40%, Day 30 retention >25%
  • Viral Coefficient: Each user invites 0.5+ friends (organic growth)

Why This Works (The Core Psychology)

  1. Solves Real Guilt: Parents feel terrible about unused memberships. This app removes shame by motivating action.
  2. Simple Visibility: You don't need AI or GPS. Just showing "4 months since last visit" + "cost per visit" creates behavior change.
  3. Social Proof: Sharing becomes natural because it's genuinely good news ("We're finally using our memberships!").
  4. Friction Reduction: Weekend reminders + weather + hours remove decision-making burden.
  5. Habit Loop: Reminder → Go → Mark → See savings → Celebrate. Each step reinforces the next.
  6. Scalable TAM: Every family with kids has memberships they're not using. Not a niche. A universal problem.
