## I. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

### A. The Community Property Interest

Your former spouse, **Donna Elizabeth McCaa**, passed away on **August 13, 2020**, without a will. At the time of your marriage dissolution, you owned stock in LandRay Technology, Inc. Under **California Family Code §2640 and community property law**, Ms. Donna McCaa retained an interest in one-half (50%) of any appreciation or proceeds from that stock during the marriage, notwithstanding your subsequent divorce. This is not disputed—you have acknowledged this entitlement.

### B. Donna's Express Intent to Bequeath Her Interest to Ms. McCaa

On multiple occasions spanning from **2015 through August 2020** (days before her death), Donna Elizabeth McCaa made clear, repeated, and unequivocal oral statements to our client expressing her intent to bequeath her entire interest in the LandRay proceeds to Ms. McCaa. Specifically:

1. In **2015**, Donna informed Ms. McCaa that she was having a will drafted naming Ms. McCaa and Richard Largay as her sole heirs, and that Donna intended her entire LandRay interest to pass to Ms. McCaa.

2. Donna sent you (Mr. Compaglia) an **email in or around 2015** explicitly declaring her intent to bequeath her LandRay interest to Ms. McCaa. Ms. McCaa was included on the blind copy (bcc) of this communication. While that specific email has not been located, its existence is corroborated by this communication chain.

3. **Over subsequent years**, Donna repeatedly reiterated to Ms. McCaa her intent that the LandRay shares should go to Ms. McCaa, including the statement that if Richard Largay predeceased her, Ms. McCaa would be the **sole beneficiary** of the LandRay interest.

4. **Directly from you**: You have personally confirmed to Ms. McCaa that Donna's stated intent was to leave her LandRay interest to Ms. McCaa. This confirmation is material evidence of the promise's authenticity.

5. **Days before her death** (approximately **August 10-13, 2020**), Donna spoke with you about LandRay, and afterward told Ms. McCaa that you had reconfirmed the value of the shares at approximately **$6-8 million**.

## II. LEGAL BASIS

### A. California Probate Code §21700 — Oral Promises to Make Bequests

California Probate Code §21700(a) permits enforcement of an oral promise to make a bequest through clear and convincing evidence of an agreement or promise between the decedent and the claimant, **or** between the decedent and a third party for the claimant's benefit.

**Cal. Prob. Code §21700(a)(4)** — An agreement between the decedent and the claimant to make a bequest, evidenced by:
- Clear and convincing evidence
- In the decedent's handwriting, OR
- Confirmed by credible evidence corroborating the agreement

**Cal. Prob. Code §21700(a)(5)** — An agreement between the decedent and another person (here: Mr. Compaglia) that benefits the claimant, similarly corroborated.

Ms. McCaa satisfies both prongs:

1. **Direct promises to Ms. McCaa** (§21700(a)(4)): Donna made repeated, clear, and unambiguous oral statements over five years expressing her intent to bequeath her LandRay interest to Ms. McCaa. These statements are consistent, remembered by Ms. McCaa with specificity, and corroborated by contemporaneous written evidence (the 2015 email, even if not currently in possession).

2. **Confirmation from Mr. Compaglia** (§21700(a)(5)): You, Mr. Compaglia, have personally confirmed to Ms. McCaa that Donna expressed this intent to you. Your confirmation serves as third-party corroboration that substantially elevates the credibility and clarity of the promise.

### B. Community Property Entitlement

Regardless of probate claims, Donna's one-half interest in LandRay proceeds stems from **community property law** and constitutes a real property right. Upon her death, that right passes to her heirs or estate. Ms. McCaa, as the intended beneficiary of that specific interest, has a claim to enforce it.
