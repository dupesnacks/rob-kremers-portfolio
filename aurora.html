TuSimple: Brand Marketing Toolkit

Brand Systems / Asset Management / Internal Enablement

The Challenge: TuSimple was scaling rapidly with distributed teams, external agency partners, and cross-functional stakeholders (marketing, safety, government relations) all creating content simultaneously. Without a central, organized source of truth for brand assets‚Äîlogos, photography standards, design guidelines, iconography‚Äîthe brand was at risk of fragmentation. Every team was making ad-hoc decisions about how to represent the company, leading to inconsistency, wasted time, and rework.

What I Built: A comprehensive Brand Marketing Toolkit that served as the single source of truth for all brand assets and usage guidelines. The toolkit organized:

  • Brand Assets: Logo variations, brand patterns, color palettes, and typography standards‚Äîall properly versioned and documented.
  • Photography Standards: Organized folders for truck imagery (on-road, facility, close-ups), with detailed descriptions of what each category was designed for and how to use them in different contexts.
  • Iconography & Diagrams: A library of visual elements for presentations, website design, and marketing materials‚Äîensuring consistency across all touchpoints.
  • Usage Guidelines: Clear documentation on when and how to use each asset, color combinations, sizing requirements, and what NOT to do.

Why It Mattered (Internal Efficiency): Before the toolkit, every request for brand assets meant hunting through shared drives or asking the design team. After launch, teams had instant access to exactly what they needed, properly organized and ready to deploy. This cut approval cycles from days to hours and eliminated the constant back-and-forth about brand guidelines.

Cross-Functional Collaboration: The toolkit became the bridge between marketing, design, safety, and government relations teams. When external agencies needed brand guidelines or internal teams were building presentations, they had a definitive reference. This reduced rework, ensured consistency, and made it easy for non-designers to make on-brand decisions independently.

Why It Matters for Aurora: As Aurora scales, you'll face the exact same challenge. Multiple teams, external partners, distributed execution‚Äîall needing to represent the brand consistently. A well-structured toolkit isn't just nice to have. It's how you maintain brand integrity while enabling speed. This work proves I can build the systems that let teams move fast without sacrificing consistency.
