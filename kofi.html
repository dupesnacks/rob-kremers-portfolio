The Problem: Why Alcon Isn't Where It Should Be

Alcon is a leader in eye care. Their contact lens business is strong ($707M in Q3 2024, +6% YoY). Their product innovation is consistent. But in the digital optometry market, they're invisible.

5 Reasons Why

1. Wrong Positioning

MARLO is positioned as a "contact lens ordering platform." It should be positioned as "the operating system for independent optometry practices."

2. No Consumer Brand

Patients don't know MARLO exists because Alcon doesn't market to patients. But here's the thing: they don't need to. They need to market to ECPs—and they're barely doing that.

3. Weak Distribution

MARLO adoption is 1M patients (impressive), but how many of the ~40,000 independent optometry practices in North America are actually using it? Unknown. Likely <10%.

4. Underestimated Competition

While Alcon sleeps, private equity is consolidating the independent practice market. EyeCare Partners grew to 671 offices in 12 months. Independent ECPs are running out of time to digitize.

5. Unclear Value Proposition

Alcon says "MARLO helps you keep reorders." Practices need to hear: "MARLO helps you compete with Warby Parker and Zenni without losing your identity or your revenue."

Why MARLO is a Hidden Gem

The Data Proves It Works

1 million patients enrolled in MARLO (significant for an invisible product)

90%+ would reorder again through MARLO (exceptional loyalty)

30%+ revenue increase for participating practices (hard proof of value)

Pilot program since 2019, now expanding (Android app, continuous updates)

Feature Set

  • 24/7 contact lens ordering (routed through practice)
  • Exam reminders & reorder notifications
  • Doctor branding (patient sees practice name, not Alcon)
  • Patient education content
  • Vision screener with Apple Health integration
  • Practice workflow management

Why It Works

  • Solves the core problem: Practices keep patient relationships while offering convenience
  • Aligns incentives: Practices benefit (revenue protection), Alcon benefits (contact lens sales)
  • No competition: Independent practices have nowhere else to go for this
  • Proven ROI: 30% revenue lift is measurable, repeatable, and hard to ignore

Why It's Invisible

  • Alcon doesn't market it to ECPs (no trade show presence, limited PR)
  • No brand awareness among patients (but that's OK—they don't need it)
  • Positioned as a "feature" not a "strategy" (should be "your digital survival kit")
  • Underestimating the consolidation threat (acting like independent practices aren't disappearing)
