# The Real Pitch: Why Your App Actually Wins

## The MARLO Parallel (And Why It's Not Perfect)

### MARLO's Problem
**Optometry:**
- Patients were buying contact lenses online (Warby Parker, Zenni, etc.)
- Optometrists lost direct revenue on lenses
- Solution: MARLO kept patients buying lenses from the optometrist instead

### The Dental Parallel (But Different)

**Dentistry has TWO problems, not one:**

1. **Revenue leakage to DTC/e-commerce** (smaller problem)
   - Patients buy whitening kits from Amazon instead of practice
   - Patients order retainers from mail-order instead of asking dentist
   - Similar to MARLO's problem, but smaller share of revenue

2. **Patient switching to DSO chains** (BIGGER problem)
   - Patients switch to Aspen Dental, Heartland, etc. because they:
     - Can book appointments online (independents: no)
     - Get faster responses (independents: wife answering phone)
     - Can reschedule easily (independents: call and wait)
     - Have app/digital experience (independents: nothing)
   - This is NOT a revenue leakage problem
   - This is a **COMPETITIVE POSITIONING problem**

## What The Research Actually Shows

### Patient Behavior
- **41% of new patients are lost** (ADA data)
- **Patients drop off until you add online scheduling**
- **Patients choose chains because of convenience and responsiveness**, not just price

### What DSO Chains Have That Independents Don't
1. Professional booking systems (online scheduling, no hold times)
2. Multiple location convenience
3. Digital communication (text/app)
4. Perceived "modern" experience
5. Operational efficiency (no one person overwhelmed)

### What Independents Actually Have (Advantage)
1. Genuine relationships with patients
2. Consistent dentist (not rotating providers)
3. Clinical autonomy and care quality
4. Community trust
5. Ability to build loyalty (if they had the tools)

## The REAL Business Case

### It's Not: "Stop patients from going to DSOs"
*(You can't. DSO market growing 17.5% CAGR. Existential trend.)*

### It's Actually: "Don't Lose Patients You Already Have"

**The Problem Statement (To A Dentist):**

> "You're losing patients—not because your clinical care is bad, but because patients don't FEEL taken care of. They can't book online. Their appointment gets forgotten. They reschedule by calling and waiting. They need a whitening kit and don't think to ask you, so they buy from Amazon. Meanwhile, the DSO across town has an app, texts reminders, and makes everything frictionless. Your patients switch not because DSOs are better clinicians—but because they FEEL more modern and responsive. You're losing patients you could keep."

### The Solution (What Your App Does)

**For the Patient:**
- Frictionless experience (online booking, SMS reminders, easy rescheduling)
- Convenient access to products (whitening, retainers, care items)
- Relationship building (personalized messages, follow-ups, recalls)
- Loyalty (they feel taken care of)

**For the Practice:**
- Operational relief (wife not drowning in phone calls)
- Revenue capture (consumables, fewer no-shows)
- Reduced patient churn (competitive with chains)
- Growth through retention (existing patients = higher LTV than new)

## The Financial Model (What Actually Matters)

### DSO Threat Analysis
- You can't stop them
- You CAN keep patients from switching in the first place
- You CAN capture revenue before it leaks

### Three Revenue Streams (In Order of Importance)

**1. Patient Retention (BIGGEST impact)**
- TFD baseline: 150 active patients, ~$1.2M annual revenue
- Patient loss rate: 15-20% per year (industry standard)
- = 22-30 patients lost per year = $220K-360K lost revenue
- Your app: Reduce loss rate to 5-10% (modest improvement)
- = Recovery of $110K-180K in retained revenue
- **On 100 practices: $11M-18M in recovered revenue (HUGE)**

**2. Appointment Adherence (MEDIUM impact)**
- Current no-show rate: 15-25%
- Cost per no-show: $100-200 lost revenue
- Your app: Reduce to 10-15% (automated reminders)
- = $50-100 recovered per no-show
- = 20-30 fewer no-shows per month per practice
- = $1,000-3,000 recovered per month per practice

**3. Consumables Revenue (LOWEST baseline, highest margin)**
- Current: Patient buys elsewhere (Amazon, mail-order)
- With app: Capture 30-40% of what was being lost
- = $200-300/month per practice
- = Better margins (40-50%) than other revenue

### Total Impact Per Practice
- Patient retention + appointment adherence: $2,000-4,000/month
- Consumables: $200-300/month
- **Total value: $2,200-4,300/month = $26K-52K/year per practice**

Vs. your cost:
- App fee + consumables commission: $2-3K/year
- **Net ROI: 10-20x**

## Why This Actually Works (vs. Copycat Apps)

### You're Not Selling Software
You're selling: **"Keep your patients from switching to chains + make operations easier + capture leaking revenue"**

This is a **defensive play**, not an offensive one.

- MARLO: "Stop patients from buying contacts elsewhere"
- Your app: "Stop patients from switching to DSO chains + capture products revenue"

### Why Dentists Actually Buy This
1. **Existential threat** - They're aware DSOs are winning. They want to compete.
2. **Operational desperation** - Wife is overwhelmed. Solutions that reduce her burden = instant value.
3. **Proven ROI** - "You'll recover $20-50K in patient retention and no-shows" is a concrete promise.
4. **Low risk** - Free trial or 30-day pilot = easy entry.

### Why This Is Better Than Building For Consumers
- Consumer apps (for patients) are weak leverage. Patients don't pay.
- Practice apps (for dentists) have strong leverage. Dentists have money and pain.
- Direct relationship with paying customer (practice owner) = stickiness.
