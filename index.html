My Background (The Throughline)

I started in traditional brand marketing at Columbia Sportswear, where I built in-store communications and retail activation strategies. It taught me the power of clarity at point of purchase — when a customer is standing in front of your product, everything you've done in marketing either works or doesn't.

Then I moved to adidas, where I managed the Amazon launch and wholesale growth strategy. This was the inflection point: I learned that brand marketing and revenue are not separate things. A great brand strategy means nothing if it doesn't drive sell-through and adoption. I learned to think in terms of channel dynamics, retail partnerships, and measurable performance.

At Nike, I built global retail brand systems. This was massive in scope — coordinating brand execution across stores, regions, and dozens of wholesale partners. The challenge wasn't creativity; it was structure. How do you give a global organization a clear framework for execution? How do you make consistency possible at scale?

Then everything changed. I went to Tesla as a frontline sales team member, then moved into product communications and sales enablement. It was deliberately humbling. I needed to understand what happens when brand meets reality — how a customer experiences your product, what questions they have, what really matters to them. This reset how I think about everything.

Most recently, I've been at TuSimple and CreateAI, leading technical storytelling and positioning for autonomous vehicles and generative AI. This taught me how to translate cutting-edge innovation into compelling narratives for investors, customers, and the market. When your product is genuinely new, clarity becomes your competitive advantage.

How I Think About Work

Start With Strategy, Not Tactics

Every project begins with clarity: What's the actual problem? What are we trying to achieve? What's the market opportunity? Only after those questions are answered do we move to execution. Too many marketing teams skip this step.

Build Systems, Not Campaigns

I think in frameworks. A good system works across channels, teams, and geographies. It's repeatable. It scales. A campaign is a moment; a system is a permanent capability. The goal is always to build the latter.

Connect Marketing to Revenue

Marketing's job isn't to look good—it's to drive business results. This means thinking about sell-through, adoption, channel health, and measurable outcomes. It means understanding how sales and operations actually work.

Lead Across Silos

The best marketing decisions require input from product, sales, operations, and customer teams. But someone has to connect those dots. That's often me. I'm comfortable being the person who says "here's what needs to happen" and then leads the organization to make it real.

Clarity Over Complexity

If you can't explain something simply, you don't understand it well enough. Whether it's a brand system, a product story, or a go-to-market plan, my job is to take complexity and make it clear, compelling, and actionable.

Measure What Matters

Data without context is noise. I focus on metrics that actually matter: sell-through, adoption, revenue impact, customer acquisition cost. Numbers should tell a story and inform decisions.
