How I Think About Work

Start With Strategy, Not Tactics

Every project begins with clarity: What's the actual problem? What are we trying to achieve? What's the market opportunity? Only after those questions are answered do we move to execution. Too many marketing teams skip this step.

Build Systems, Not Campaigns

I think in frameworks. A good system works across channels, teams, and geographies. It's repeatable. It scales. A campaign is a moment; a system is a permanent capability. The goal is always to build the latter.

Connect Marketing to Revenue

Marketing's job isn't to look good. It's to drive business results. This means thinking about sell-through, adoption, channel health, and measurable outcomes. It means understanding how sales and operations actually work.

Lead Across Silos

The best marketing decisions require input from product, sales, operations, and customer teams. But someone has to connect those dots. That's often me. I'm comfortable being the person who says "here's what needs to happen" and then leads the organization to make it real.

Clarity Over Complexity

If you can't explain something simply, you don't understand it well enough. Whether it's a brand system, a product story, or a go-to-market plan, my job is to take complexity and make it clear, compelling, and actionable.

Measure What Matters

Data without context is noise. I focus on metrics that actually matter: sell-through, adoption, revenue impact, customer acquisition cost. Numbers should tell a story and inform decisions.

Selected Work & Impact

Nike Global Basketball Retail Brand Playbook
Brand Strategy / Retail Systems / Global Execution

The Challenge: Nike Basketball needed a comprehensive, globally scalable playbook that could guide retail partners, wholesalers, and market teams on how to execute brand strategy with consistency across different regions and store environments.

What I Built: A structured playbook covering four core pillars (Category, Product, Service, and Environment) with detailed guidance on positioning, product presentation, service standards, and store design. The playbook served as the foundation for all DTC retail execution worldwide.

Impact: A living system that set the standard for how Nike Basketball should show up in retail, enabling markets and partners to execute with clarity without sacrificing local relevance.

adidas DSG Fixture System Rollout
Retail Execution / Account Management / Shop-in-Shop Strategy

The Challenge: adidas developed a new fixture system to modernize how the brand appears in wholesale retail. The North American rollout required adapting this system for Dick's Sporting Goods (DSG) while balancing adidas' global brand vision with DSG's retail environment, creative requirements, and operational constraints.

What I Led: End-to-end North American rollout of the fixture system at DSG. Partnered with global and local teams to translate the fixture concept into a tailored shop-in-shop experience. Managed prototyping, vendor relationships, and strategic fixture selection to ensure DSG could bring adidas' brand stories to life while staying operationally feasible.

Impact: Successfully deployed across DSG locations, creating a premium adidas brand experience within a multi-brand retail environment. Demonstrated how to adapt global brand systems for local retail partners without compromising brand integrity.

adidas DSG "Earn Your Spotlight" Campaign
Campaign Strategy / Multi-Channel Activation / Retail + Digital Integration

The Challenge: Launch exclusive adidas training apparel at Dick's Sporting Goods with a campaign that would drive both emotional connection and commercial impact. The message had to resonate with today's athletes: people who seek validation and recognition beyond traditional metrics.

The Insight: Modern athletes don't just chase wins. They chase personal moments that matter, their own "spotlight moments." This became the campaign foundation.

What I Developed: A comprehensive multi-channel strategy across social, digital, in-store, and live activations. Featured top-tier athletes and influencers to drive aspiration and authenticity. Planned a 10-city nationwide activation tour bringing the campaign to life in DSG retail hubs and local communities.

Results: 55M+ impressions, 2.7M video views, $2.6M attributed revenue, 460K store visits, 35K e-commerce orders. Drove significant scale while maintaining retail partnership integrity.

Damian Lillard Dame 6 Launch Film
Brand Storytelling / Content / Identity Positioning

The Concept: A launch film for the Dame 6 shoe that goes beyond product specs to tell Damian Lillard's identity. The piece highlights who he is beyond basketball: a son, father, rapper, and leader rooted in the cities that shaped him (Oakland, Ogden, Portland).

What Makes It Work: Rather than focusing on shoe features or performance metrics, the film reflects the campaign's core message: he's more than just an NBA player. It's brand storytelling that connects the product to authentic human narrative.

Impact: A masterclass in how to build brand around identity, not just product. The film anchors the entire Damian Lillard brand ecosystem.

adidas D Rose 10 Launch Film
Product Launch / Archival Storytelling / Retail & Social Content

The Concept: A stop-motion inspired film for the D Rose 10 launch that moves beyond product features to celebrate Derrick Rose's legacy. Using archival stills from his adidas journey, the piece creates a visual timeline of his career accolades and signature moments.

The Approach: Rather than traditional product launch content, this film treats the shoe as the culmination of a legacy. Each frame from Derrick's career (the accolades, the signature plays, the evolution) builds toward the design itself. It's about honoring what came before.

Distribution: Deployed at retail in China and across social media, reaching a global audience of fans who grew up with Derrick Rose and new audiences discovering his impact.

Impact: Proved that product launch content doesn't need to be about specs. When you connect the product to authentic human legacy, you create emotional resonance that drives both retail impact and social conversation.

TuSimple: My Story Trailer Campaign
Internal Storytelling / Brand Humanization / Documentary

The Challenge: TuSimple was operating in a complex, novel space (autonomous trucking) where the company narrative often got lost in technical specs and investor decks. The brand needed humanization: a way to show the actual people building this technology and why they're committed to it.

The Campaign: Built a documentary-style campaign called "My TuSimple Story" designed to put the spotlight on the people who do the work: engineers, drivers, operations teams. Through intimate, authentic storytelling, the campaign showed the inner workings of the company and the human motivations behind the innovation.

Outcome: While the project was ultimately pivoted due to business priorities shifting, it demonstrated how to translate a technical company into a human one. This approach became foundational for how I later approached the CreateAI positioning work.

TuSimple Domain Controller Product Launch
Product Launch / Paid Social & Search / Technical Positioning

The Challenge: Launch a complex piece of autonomous vehicle hardware (the TuSimple Domain Controller) to a B2B audience of fleet operators, automotive OEMs, and enterprise customers. The product is technically sophisticated (NVIDIA DRIVE, Infineon architecture, 254 TOPS GPU performance) but abstract to non-engineers. How do you communicate hardware capability in a way that resonates with decision-makers?

What I Built: A landing page and paid campaign strategy that stacked credibility signals. Hero section with clear value prop ("Enable SAE Level 2+ to Level 4 Autonomous Solutions"). Technical specs for decision-makers (CPU/GPU performance, camera/LIDAR capacity, connectivity options). "What's Inside?" deep dive showing real components (NVIDIA, Infineon, GNX/QNX) to signal engineering rigor. Reliability credentials (ISO 16750 certification). Contact-driven lead gen model, not e-commerce.

Paid Strategy: Launched search and social campaigns targeting fleet operators, AV teams, and enterprise customers. Messaging tested value prop variations (performance vs. safety vs. integration readiness) across channels.

Impact: High-intent traffic from qualified audience. Landing page positioned the Domain Controller as enterprise-grade, not another tech product. Demonstrated how to translate hardware complexity into credible, structured narrative for B2B buyers.
