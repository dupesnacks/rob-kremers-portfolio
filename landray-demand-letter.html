I. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

This is not disputed—you have acknowledged this entitlement.

II. LEGAL BASIS

A. California Probate Code §21700 — Oral Promises to Make Bequests

California Probate Code §21700(a) permits enforcement of an oral promise to make a bequest through clear and convincing evidence of an agreement or promise between the decedent and the claimant, or between the decedent and a third party for the claimant's benefit.

Cal. Prob. Code §21700(a)(4) — An agreement between the decedent and the claimant to make a bequest, evidenced by:

  • Clear and convincing evidence
  • In the decedent's handwriting, OR
  • Confirmed by credible evidence corroborating the agreement

Cal. Prob. Code §21700(a)(5) — An agreement between the decedent and another person (here: Mr. Compaglia) that benefits the claimant, similarly corroborated.

Ms. McCaa satisfies both prongs:

  1. Direct promises to Ms. McCaa (§21700(a)(4)): Donna made repeated, clear, and unambiguous oral statements over five years expressing her intent to bequeath her LandRay interest to Ms. McCaa. These statements are consistent, remembered by Ms. McCaa with specificity, and corroborated by contemporaneous written evidence (the 2015 email, even if not currently in possession).
  2. Confirmation from Mr. Compaglia (§21700(a)(5)): You, Mr. Compaglia, have personally confirmed to Ms. McCaa that Donna expressed this intent to you. Your confirmation serves as third-party corroboration that substantially elevates the credibility and clarity of the promise.

B. Community Property Entitlement

Regardless of probate claims, Donna's one-half interest in LandRay proceeds stems from community property law and constitutes a real property right. Upon her death, that right passes to her heirs or estate. Ms. McCaa, as the intended beneficiary of that specific interest, has a claim to enforce it.
