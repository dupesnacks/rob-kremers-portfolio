🤖 AI Data Scarcity Thesis

The Setup

Two simultaneous structural forces converge: AI models need vast quantities of high-quality, authentic human-generated text (synthetic content degrades model performance), and community-based advertising delivers superior ROI vs. algorithmic feeds.
Model Collapse Problem: AI models trained on AI-generated content degrade in quality. Authentic human-generated content becomes the scarce, premium input.
Dynamic Pricing Power: Companies owning authentic conversation data can charge for real-time access. This revenue carries near-100% margins because users create the content.
Community Advertising Efficiency: 40% of Reddit conversations are commercial; 84% of shoppers feel more confident after researching on Reddit. Intent-based community advertising outperforms algorithmic feeds.

⚒️ Critical Minerals Supercycle

The Setup

Critical minerals have become the defining geopolitical battleground. Demand is surging (EVs, AI, wind, defense) while supply is constrained by China's dominance of processing (40–90% market share), export controls, and 10–20 year timelines to bring new mines online. US policy response = most aggressive minerals policy since Cold War.

Five Structural Tailwinds

1. Mandatory Supply Chain Decoupling: US procurement rules exclude Chinese-origin minerals from defense/federal contracts. Creates captive, price-inelastic demand for Western producers.
2. Explosive Demand: EVs use 6× more minerals than conventional cars. Wind turbines require 9× more than gas plants. AI data centers consuming massive copper/REE/specialty metals.
3. Government as Anchor Buyer: $14.8B EXIM Bank Letters of Interest | $10B+ strategic reserve (Project Vault) | Direct equity stakes in multiple miners. Provides downside protection and offtake certainty.
4. Supply Cannot Respond: Typical mine takes 10–20 years discovery→production. Even with record investment, meaningful supply years away. Supply inelasticity during demand surge = prolonged cycle.
5. China's Export Controls Are Permanent: Licensing system on 12+ REEs = structural policy, not negotiating chip. Remains active even during diplomatic pauses.

🥇 Precious Metals Bull Cycle

The Setup

Bullish, macro-driven thesis predicated on Federal Reserve rate cuts creating tailwinds for gold, silver, and mining equities. Falling real yields reduce opportunity cost of non-yielding gold. Silver carries dual tailwind: monetary demand + industrial demand (solar, EVs, AI data centers). Record central bank buying + structural silver deficit drive extended bull cycle.

Five Core Drivers

1. Falling Real Yields: Fed rate cuts compress real yields, removing opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold. Historical pattern: rate cuts → rotation out of bonds → gold inflows
2. Weaker US Dollar: Fed easing weakens dollar, making metals cheaper for global buyers. Establishes price floor
3. Inflation Hedge Demand: Rate cuts amid sticky inflation signal currency debasement risk. Drives institutional and central bank buying
4. Miner Margin Expansion: Lower cost of capital + higher bullion prices against fixed operating costs = 2–3× leveraged profit moves relative to metal price itself
5. Silver's Dual Tailwind: Lower rates stimulate capex in solar, EVs, AI data centers — all silver-intensive. Silver carries unique double leverage: monetary asset + industrial demand

AKAM — Misunderstood Transition Story

The Thesis: CDN Legacy → Security-First Cloud Infrastructure

Market Mispricing: Akamai valued as slow-growth CDN (~36x P/E) when actually transforming into security-first, edge-cloud infrastructure provider

Three Key Business Drivers:

1. Cybersecurity (~40% of revenue, growing 12%+ YoY):
  • Highest-margin segment | Current growth engine
  • Covers: Web application protection, API security, bot management, account protection
  • Structural Advantage: Sitting at internet perimeter allows threat detection BEFORE reaching customer infrastructure (advantage vs. Cloudflare, Palo Alto, CrowdStrike)
  • API security TAM alone projected $7B+ by 2028 (30%+ annual growth)

2. Cloud Infrastructure Services (CIS) (~20% of revenue, growing 25%+):
  • Most transformative driver. In Q4 2025, signed $200M multi-year CIS commitment; first Inference Cloud sold out immediately
  • Purchased 11,000+ Nvidia Blackwell GPUs to scale offering
  • Key Differentiation: GPU compute deployed across 4,100 edge locations (vs. hyperscaler centralized data centers). DRAMATICALLY LOWER LATENCY for AI inference workloads
  • Structural advantage over AWS, Azure, Google Cloud for real-time AI applications
  • If CIS grows to 30%+ of revenue by 2027 = material valuation re-rating justified

3. Valuation Disconnect & Re-Rating Potential:
  • Pure-play security comps trade at 40-60x P/E (CrowdStrike 60x+, Palo Alto 45x+)
  • AKAM at 36x P/E reflects CDN drag; as revenue mix shifts toward security/cloud, multiple expansion is key upside lever
  • Strong FCF ($1B+ annually) + buybacks provide EPS support during transition
  • Low beta (~0.65) offers portfolio stability not found in pure AI/cloud names

What Confirms Thesis: CIS revenue acceleration | Security margin expansion | Above-consensus guidance

Key Tension

Against the Thesis: Hold consensus among analysts | Below-consensus margin guidance | Delivery revenue in secular decline | Execution risk of three simultaneous business transitions against better-capitalized competitors (Cloudflare, AWS, CrowdStrike)
Bottom Line: May 7 earnings are pivotal. Thesis rests on proving CIS is real revenue contributor, not vaporware
