03b. Another Builder Story

From One App to Two

While building Dupe Snacks, I started thinking about another problem: How do you help kids expand their palate when they have selective eating patterns?

Real problem: Kids with sensory sensitivities get stuck eating the same 5 foods. Trying anything new feels impossible.

The idea: A game that turns eating into an adventure. Explore zones, unlock stuff, get recommendations based on what your kid actually likes.

The trick: "Bridge foods." We suggest foods that are similar to what they already eat (familiar flavors/textures) but introduce one new thing. Food science meets psychology meets game design.

The Feature Set

Feature set:

  • Sensory Profiles: Tracks what textures/flavors the kid actually likes
  • Smart Recommendations: AI suggests the next food based on their preferences
  • Gamification: Quests, badges, streaks, progression. Makes it fun.
  • Parent Dashboard: See what your kid is exploring, what they're ready for next
  • Community: Challenges with other kids using the app

Why This Matters

This is way more complex than Dupe Snacks. AI recommendations, multi-player gamification, parent-child accounts, analytics, behavioral design. The stuff you'd normally need a team for.

Building it the same way: with Rork, no coding, moving fast.

The point: This isn't just speed. It's complexity. I can build sophisticated products that would need a whole team. Solo. Rork makes that possible.

Two apps. Two different problems. Two different complexity levels. All Rorked.
