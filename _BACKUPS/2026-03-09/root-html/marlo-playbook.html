Patient Communication Strategy

The Objective

Transform patients from one-time appointment visitors into engaged participants in their eye care journey. Drive higher appointment attendance, contact lens reorders through MARLO, and long-term practice loyalty.

The Problem MARLO Solves

  • Appointment no-shows: Estimated 20-25% no-show rate in optometry (industry average)
  • Contact lens leakage: Patients reorder from Zenni/Warby instead of through practice
  • Relationship gaps: Patients feel no connection between visits; consider switching practices
  • Patient education deficit: Patients don't understand eye health, postpone exams, ignore prescriptions

Patient Communication Framework: 5-Channel Omnichannel Approach

SMS (Text Message) Appointment reminders, urgent updates, time-sensitive offers 1 day before, 2 hours before appointment 98% open rate, 45-60% response rate

Patient Segmentation & Personalized Messaging

Key Insight: Personalized SMS messages boost response rates by up to 70% compared to generic messages. Segment patients by behavior and tailor messaging accordingly.

Segment 1: Appointment-Focused

Who: Patients who haven't booked their annual eye exam (overdue)

Message: "Sarah, it's been [X] months since your last exam. Your eyes change—let's schedule your check-up. Your frames style: [style]. [Book Now button]"

Channel: SMS first (urgent), then email, then push if no response in 3 days

Personalization: Use patient name, past frame choices, timing since last visit

Segment 2: Contact Lens Reorder

Who: Active contact lens wearers with saved prescriptions in MARLO

Message: "Time to reorder! Your Bausch + Lomb Biotrue [prescription]. Same lenses, one-click. Your doc approved. [Reorder button]"

Channel: Push notification (in-app first), SMS if no action in 1 day

Personalization: Pre-fill lens type, prescription, pricing

Offer: Loyalty bonus ("5th order free") to drive repeat behavior

Segment 3: Education-Focused

Who: New patients or those flagged for educational needs (e.g., dry eye, presbyopia)

Message: Email: "Dry Eye? Here's Why It Happens & How to Manage It" + actionable tips + link to schedule follow-up

Channel: Email with option to engage more via SMS/push

Personalization: Reference patient's specific condition or recent diagnosis

Segment 4: Lapsed Patients

Who: Patients who haven't engaged with MARLO or practice in 3+ months

Message: "We miss you! Your doctor wants to know how your eyes are doing. [Book Now] or chat with us anytime."

Channel: SMS (personalized), then email, then in-app message

Goal: Re-engagement, not aggressive selling

Communication Best Practices (HIPAA-Safe)

  • Personalization: Use patient name, history, preferences. Avoid generic templates.
  • Timing: Send SMS reminders 24 hours and 2 hours before appointments (proven optimal windows)
  • Frequency: Max 2-3 messages per week to active patients; respect preferences
  • HIPAA Compliance: Never include medical details in SMS/email headers; all messages must allow opt-out
  • Mobile-First: 80%+ of patients read emails on mobile; ensure responsive design
  • Clear CTAs: Every message needs one action (book, reorder, survey, read more)
  • Data Privacy: Only share necessary details; always get consent before enrolling in communication programs

✅ 2.8X increase in member appointments
✅ 70% reduction in operational costs (fewer no-shows)
✅ Improved patient satisfaction (fewer missed care opportunities)

ECP Marketing Strategy (B2B)

The Objective

Accelerate MARLO adoption among the remaining 30,000 independent optometry practices (75% of the market not yet using MARLO). Move from 25% market penetration to 55%+ within 18-24 months through targeted B2B marketing.

Why ECP Marketing is Different from Consumer Marketing

Factor Consumer (Patient) Marketing B2B (ECP) Marketing
Decision Maker Individual patient Practice owner + optometrist (multiple stakeholders)
Decision Criteria Convenience, price, brand trust ROI, revenue impact, ease of use, support, compliance
Sales Cycle Days to weeks Weeks to months (requires demos, trials, executive buy-in)
Trust Drivers Reviews, brand, recommendations Peer endorsements, case studies, ROI proof, industry credibility
Key Channels Social media, search, reviews Peer networks, professional associations, thought leadership, account-based marketing

ECP Marketing Framework: 4-Pillar Approach

Pillar 1: Peer Referral Program (FASTEST GROWTH)

Strategy: Use existing 10,000 MARLO practices as sales force. Pay them to refer other practices.

Mechanism: $500-1,500 bounty per referred practice that signs 12-month contract

Why it works: ECPs trust other ECPs more than marketing. Peer endorsement is the strongest signal.

Execution:

  • Create referral portal in MARLO practice dashboard
  • Provide pre-built pitch + ROI calculator for referring practices
  • Track referrals and payouts automatically
  • Gamify with leaderboards ("Top Referrers" = prestige)

Expected outcome: 10,000 practices × 2-3 referrals each = 20,000-30,000 new practices in 12 months

Pillar 2: Professional Association Strategy

Strategy: Secure endorsements and premium positioning from AAO, state optometry societies, and regional networks.

Mechanism:

  • Sponsorships of optometry conferences (Optometry's Meeting, SECO, state conferences)
  • Educational webinars: "How to Compete with Consolidation Using MARLO" (position as survival tool)
  • Feature in association publications and newsletters
  • Speaking slots at conferences (position Alcon exec as industry expert)

Why it works: ECPs look to professional bodies for guidance. Association endorsement = credibility

Expected outcome: Reach 50,000+ optometrists at major conferences; case study features in 3+ publications

Pillar 3: Account-Based Marketing (ABM) for Regional Groups

Strategy: Identify and target high-value practice groups with personalized campaigns.

Target profiles:

  • Large independent practice groups (5-20 doctors)
  • Practices recently hit by consolidation threats in their region
  • Practices currently using outdated software (churn opportunity)

Execution:

  1. Identify target practices by region and size
  2. Personalized outreach: "Saw consolidation happening in [city]. Here's how other practices in [region] stayed independent with MARLO."
  3. Executive briefing: Regional case study + ROI model specific to their market
  4. Free trial + dedicated onboarding

Why it works: Large groups have high lifetime value; personalization shows you understand their market

Expected outcome: 2-3X higher conversion rate vs. generic campaigns; average deal value $50K-150K/year per group

Pillar 4: Thought Leadership & Content Marketing

Strategy: Position Alcon/MARLO as the authority on independent practice survival in a consolidating market.

Content pillars:

  • Research: "2024 State of Independent Optometry" (market consolidation data, survival strategies)
  • Case Studies: "[Practice Name] Increased Revenue 30% & Stopped Patient Leakage with MARLO"
  • Guides: "ECP's Survival Playbook: 7 Strategies to Compete with DTC and Chains"
  • Webinars: Monthly "Practice Accelerator Series" (live Q&A with successful MARLO practices)

Why it works: Thought leadership positions MARLO as the solution, not just a product

Channels: LinkedIn, optometry forums, association publications, direct email to target practices

Expected outcome: 3-5% of reached practices convert; builds inbound interest for sales team

Omnichannel Customer Journey (Beyond Email/SMS)

The Objective

Map the complete patient journey from initial appointment booking through lifetime relationship management. Use multiple touchpoints (app, SMS, email, push, telehealth, community) to maximize appointment attendance, contact lens reorders, and practice loyalty—ultimately increasing patient lifetime value.

The Journey Phases

PHASE 1: AWARENESS & DISCOVERY (Week -2 to 0)

Goal: Patient finds practice, learns what MARLO is, builds confidence in provider

Touchpoints:

  • Practice Website: Clear call-to-action: "Use MARLO to book your appointment"
  • Google Search: Local SEO (practice name + "eye exam near me")
  • Social Media: Practice Facebook/Instagram posts about MARLO ease
  • Direct: Word-of-mouth from friend/family (highest trust signal)

MARLO Experience: Website shows MARLO app icon, benefits ("Manage your Rx anytime", "Quick reorders", "Appointment reminders")

Success Metric: Click-through to "Download MARLO" or "Book Appointment"

PHASE 2: REGISTRATION & ONBOARDING (Week 0-1)

Goal: Patient creates account, confirms they're connected to practice, understands core features

Touchpoints:

  • In-App Onboarding: 3-screen tutorial: "Your Practice Portal" → "Book Appointments" → "Manage Rx"
  • Email: Welcome message with practice name, doctor bio, hours, contact info
  • SMS: Confirmation: "Welcome to [Practice Name]'s MARLO. Ready for your appointment?"

Success Metric: Account creation, practice verified, push notifications enabled

PHASE 3: APPOINTMENT BOOKING (Week 1)

Goal: Patient books appointment, receives confirmation, prepares for visit

Touchpoints:

  • In-App Calendar: Available times, one-tap booking
  • SMS (Day 0): "Your appointment confirmed for [date/time] with [doctor]. See you then!"
  • Email (Day 1): Pre-appointment info: "What to bring," "How to prepare," appointment details

Success Metric: Appointment booked, confirmation received, no-show rate <15%

PHASE 4: PRE-APPOINTMENT ENGAGEMENT (Days 2-4)

Goal: Keep patient engaged, reduce no-shows, prime them for exam

Touchpoints:

  • Push Notification (Day 2): "Quick question: Any vision changes since last visit?" [Survey link]
  • In-App Message (Day 3): "Here's what your doctor will check during your exam" [Educational content]
  • SMS (Day 3, 24h before): "Your appointment is tomorrow at [time]. [Directions] [Reschedule if needed]"
  • Push (2h before): Final reminder with address + parking info

Success Metric: Click-through on reminders, appointment attendance rate

PHASE 5: AT THE PRACTICE (Day 4)

Goal: Seamless in-office experience, patient sees MARLO as extension of practice

Touchpoints:

  • In-App Check-In: Patient checks in via MARLO (reduces wait)
  • Waiting Room: Monitor displays: "Your exam details saved in MARLO"
  • At Exam: Doctor references MARLO history ("I see you typically order Bausch + Lomb")
  • After Exam: Staff confirms: "Your new Rx is in MARLO. You can reorder anytime."

Success Metric: Positive exam experience, Rx saved in MARLO

PHASE 6: IMMEDIATE POST-APPOINTMENT (Days 5-7)

Goal: Drive first reorder, confirm satisfaction, reinforce loyalty

Touchpoints:

  • SMS (Day 5): "Thanks for coming! Your new Rx is saved. [Reorder Contacts] or [Order Glasses]"
  • In-App Prompt (Day 5): "Time to reorder? Your contacts are ready." [One-click order]
  • Email (Day 6): "Care tips for your new prescription" + links to educational content
  • Push (Day 7): "How do your new lenses feel? [Quick survey]"

Success Metric: Reorder conversion rate, first-reorder timing (goal: day 1-7)

PHASE 7: RETENTION & ONGOING ENGAGEMENT (Weeks 2-12)

Goal: Keep patient engaged, drive repeat reorders, build habit

Touchpoints:

  • Reorder Reminders (Day 30): SMS + Push: "Time to reorder? You last ordered [date]." [Quick reorder]
  • Educational Drip (Weekly): Email: Eye health tips, new product info, lifestyle advice
  • Community Engagement (Monthly): In-app: Patient challenges ("Track your eye health week"), Q&A with doctor
  • Loyalty Program (Ongoing): Push: "You've ordered 3 times! Earn rewards on next order."
  • Telehealth Option (On-demand): "Questions about your new Rx? Chat with [doctor's name]" [In-app chat]

Success Metric: Reorder frequency, email engagement rate, app session frequency

PHASE 8: NEXT APPOINTMENT CYCLE (Month 3-12)

Goal: Drive next annual exam, maintain ongoing relationship

Touchpoints:

  • Proactive Reminder (Day 90): SMS: "Your annual eye exam is coming up. [Book now]" (before patient thinks about it)
  • Email (Month 5): "Your eyes change. Time for your check-up." [Schedule link]
  • In-App Message (Month 6): "Your eye health matters. Let's schedule your next exam." [Doctor profile + availability]
  • SMS (Month 8, if not booked): "You're overdue! [Book appointment]"

Success Metric: Appointment booking, appointment attendance, patient lifetime value
