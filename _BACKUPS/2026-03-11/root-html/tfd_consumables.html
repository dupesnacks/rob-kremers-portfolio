💰 TFD Consumables Supply Strategy

How to Make Money on Retainers, Whitening Kits & Care Products (Without Owning a Factory)

The Problem: Alcon Owns the Supply Chain

MARLO works because Alcon manufactures contact lenses, solutions, and optics. They control the entire value chain and take 100% of the margin.

You don't have that luxury. So you need a different play.

What's Being Lost to Amazon

  • Retainers: $80-200 per unit (Vivera, Essix, generic)
  • Whitening kits: $80-300 per kit (Zoom, GLO, Opalescence)
  • Care products: $20-50/month recurring (toothbrushes, mouthwash, guards)

For a practice like TFD: $200-300/month bleeding to Amazon. Multiply that by 100 practices, and you've got an opportunity.

Three Business Models

Model 1: Affiliate Commission (Fast Start)

How it works: Partner with GLO Science, Zoom, Vivera as an affiliate. Patient buys → You get 5-15% commission.

✅ Pros

  • Zero inventory risk
  • Easy to launch
  • Fast cash flow
  • No supplier negotiations

❌ Cons

  • Lowest margin (5-10%)
  • Supplier owns relationship
  • No pricing control
  • Supplier can change terms

Revenue (TFD Example)

50 whitening kits/month × $150 × 7% = $525/month = $6,300/year

Timeline

This week. Contact GLO Science, Philips, Opalescence. Ask for affiliate program. Get links. Embed in app.

Model 2: Wholesale Partnership (Better Margins)

How it works: Buy from suppliers at wholesale (40-50% off). Sell at retail in app. You pocket the margin.

✅ Pros

  • 40-50% gross margin
  • You control pricing
  • You own customer
  • Sustainable model

❌ Cons

  • Requires capital
  • Inventory management
  • Logistics/shipping
  • Supplier negotiation

Revenue (TFD Example)

50 kits/month: Buy $75 wholesale, sell $150 retail = $3,750/month = $45,000/year (gross margin)

Timeline

Month 3. Prove affiliate model first. Once you have metrics, approach Henry Schein or Patterson Dental. Ask for practice wholesale account. Start with whitening only.

Model 3: White-Label Partnership (Best Positioning)

How it works: Partner with supplier for exclusive distribution. Co-branded products. Revenue share: 30-40% to you.

✅ Pros

  • 30-40% margins
  • Exclusive moat
  • Supplier credibility
  • Custom branding

❌ Cons

  • Hard to negotiate
  • Need proof of volume
  • Locked-in contracts
  • Requires scale first

Revenue (At Scale: 100 Practices)

100 practices × 100 kits/month × $150 × 30% = $450K/month = $5.4M/year

Timeline

Month 8-12. After running Model 2 with 5-10 practices and moving 500+ units/month, approach supplier. "I have 100 practices waiting. Want to be their exclusive whitening provider?"

The Real Play

You don't need to own manufacturing. You need to own the distribution channel and patient relationship.

Supplier makes the product. You own the practice relationship. You become the trusted middleman. Everyone wins.

By Year 2, consumables revenue becomes 20-30% of your total app business. That's your moat. That's why practices stick with you instead of switching to a competitor.
