The Problem: Why Alcon Isn't Where It Should Be

Alcon is a leader in eye care. Their contact lens business is strong ($707M in Q3 2024, +6% YoY). Their product innovation is consistent. But in the digital optometry market, they're invisible.

The Growth Gap: Why 25% Isn't 55% Yet

1. Under-Leveraged Marketing

MARLO has 10,000 practices (25% market share) with minimal marketing spend. This proves word-of-mouth is strong. But Alcon isn't amplifying this signal. They could be reaching 70% with aggressive, targeted marketing to practices.

2. Positioning is Too Narrow

MARLO is positioned as a "contact lens ordering platform." It should own the "operating system for independent optometry survival" narrative—especially as consolidation threatens practice independence.

3. Feature Set is Incomplete

MARLO works for reorders, but it's missing features that would make it the hub of practice operations: insurance verification, patient communications, revenue analytics, prescription management. These would increase switching costs and stickiness.

4. Consolidation is Accelerating Faster Than MARLO Growth

EyeCare Partners added 671 offices in 2024 alone. While MARLO grows 15-20% organically, consolidation is eating the market at 25-30% annually. They're losing the race.

5. Industry Standard Status is Missing

10,000 practices use MARLO, but it's not yet the "industry standard" like Salesforce is for CRM. Getting there requires thought leadership, partnerships, and association endorsements—things Alcon hasn't invested in.
