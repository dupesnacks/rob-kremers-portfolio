# 🎬 Sensory Galaxy Website Copy Proposal
## Strategic Reframe: Features → Emotions → Downloads

**Date:** March 11, 2026  
**Goal:** Turn website into a conversion machine for 7-day free trial signups  
**Approach:** Apply emotional-first onboarding philosophy to landing page (not everywhere, just strategic spots)

---

## THE PROBLEM WITH CURRENT COPY

### Current Issues

| Section | Current Copy | Problem | Impact |
|---------|-------------|---------|--------|
| **Hero Title** | "Turn Picky Eating Into An Adventure" | Features (gamification) not emotions | Parent doesn't see their problem solved |
| **Hero Tagline** | "A gamified app that helps kids ages 3-10 try new foods using science-backed methods" | Lists features, not outcomes | Parent thinks "cute app" not "life-changing solution" |
| **No Recognition** | Missing entirely | Parent never hears "I see your struggle" | No emotional hook, high bounce rate |
| **Planets Section** | Just lists 8 planets | Game mechanics, not parent benefit | Parent doesn't understand what this DOES |
| **Explorers Section** | "Choose your guide for an epic sensory adventure!" | Fun for kids, doesn't speak to parent | Parent is audience, not the explorer |
| **Features Section** | "Smart Analytics," "Gamified Progress," "Bridge Foods" | Jargon. No emotional context | Parent reads features, doesn't feel understood |
| **Why Parents Love** | "Science-Based," "Parent Dashboard," "Customizable" | Cold. Clinical. Not love. | Misses emotional resonance |
| **Pricing** | "Simple, transparent pricing" | Rational, not emotional | No urgency. No "why now?" |
| **No CTA Context** | Buttons say "Download" / "Start Free Trial" | No context for why to download | Low conversion (no emotional motivation) |

### Root Cause
**Current website is built for people who already know they want the app.** It explains what it is, but doesn't convince them they need it.

---

## WHAT THE APP ACTUALLY DOES (For Parents)

### The Parent's Real Problem
- **Pain:** My kid eats 5 foods. Everything else is a battle. I feel like I'm failing.
- **Emotion:** Guilt, frustration, exhaustion, isolation
- **Question:** Is this normal? What am I doing wrong? How do I fix it?

### What Sensory Galaxy Solves
1. **Validation:** You're not failing. This is real (sensory sensitivity, not pickiness)
2. **Method:** Gives you a science-backed approach (SOS method) that actually works
3. **Progress:** Shows you data proving your kid IS getting braver (removes doubt)
4. **Guilt Release:** You're not forcing/bribing anymore. You're working WITH their nervous system.
5. **Mealtimes:** Shifts from battles to celebration
6. **Community:** Realize you're not alone (1 in 3 kids have this)

### For Kids
- Makes exploring food fun (gamification, planets, explorers, badges)
- No pressure (they move at their own pace through SOS phases)
- Feels like a game, not therapy
- Real progress = motivation

### 5. HOW IT WORKS SECTION (RENAME + REORDER)

**Current:**
```
How It Works

📊 Smart Analytics
Track sensory exposure patterns, food group diversity, and color variety. 
Get personalized insights powered by AI.

🎮 Gamified Progress
Daily streaks, milestone badges, and explorer cosmetics keep kids 
engaged and motivated.

🌉 Bridge Foods
Intelligent recommendations for the next food to try, based on 
sensory profile matching.
```

**Proposed (3-Part Flow):**
```
THREE THINGS THE APP DOES FOR YOU:

1️⃣ UNDERSTAND THE PATTERN
You log what your child explores (any SOS phase counts). 
The app finds patterns:
- Which textures does my kid gravitate toward?
- What flavors are they comfortable with?
- Are they progressing, or stuck?
- What should I try next?

Parent benefit: Removes guesswork. You see proof they're getting braver.

---

2️⃣ GET SMART SUGGESTIONS
After 3-5 foods logged, the AI (trained on the SOS approach) 
recommends bridge foods—foods that match what they like, 
but introduce something new.

Example: If they like crunchy + salty crackers, 
try crunchy + salty pretzels.

Parent benefit: Every suggestion is actually doable. Less rejection.

---

3️⃣ CELEBRATE PROGRESS
Your child earns streaks, badges, and cosmetics for exploring. 
You see the data. 

Parent benefit: Proof that it's working. Guilt completely gone. 
Mealtimes shift from battles to "look how brave they're being."
```

**Why This Works:**
1. ✅ Explains features but connects to parent outcomes
2. ✅ Logical flow: log → understand → suggest → celebrate
3. ✅ Gives parent vocabulary for what's happening
4. ✅ Removes feature jargon ("Smart Analytics" → "Understand the Pattern")
5. ✅ Shows parent benefit for each feature

## TONE & VOICE THROUGHOUT

**Current Tone:** Corporate, feature-focused, clinical  
**Proposed Tone:** Warm, honest, parent-first, hopeful

**Guidelines:**
- ✅ Use "you/your" when addressing parent
- ✅ Acknowledge struggle before offering solution
- ✅ Use contractions ("it's," "don't") - conversational
- ✅ Avoid jargon until explained in context
- ✅ Balance hope with realism ("4-8 weeks," not "instant")
- ✅ Reassure parents they're not failing
- ✅ Position app as *partner*, not *solution they provide*
