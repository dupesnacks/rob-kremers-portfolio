# Onboarding Cards - Revised Copy (Card Display + Modal Pattern)

## Pattern:
```
[CARD VIEW] (Always visible)
- Headline (big, bold, cyan)
- Beautiful illustration/visual
- 1-2 line subtext (optional, gray)
- [Learn More ℹ️] CTA button (cyan text, centered)
- [Next →] button (main CTA, cyan/gold button)

[MODAL] (Tap "Learn More")
- Full detailed copy (explanation text)
- [Next →] button to close modal & proceed
```

TITLE: "What is the SOS Approach?"

BODY TEXT:
We use the Sequential Oral Sensory (SOS) Approach—the same method occupational therapists and speech therapists have used for 30+ years.

Here's how it works: Instead of pressure or bribes, we work WITH your child's nervous system.

👁️ LOOK — Getting used to seeing the food
🙌 TOUCH — Exploring textures with their hands
👃 SMELL — Experiencing the aroma
👅 LICK — Trying tiny tastes
😋 TASTE — Eating and enjoying

Each phase takes 3-10 days. No rushing. Your child's nervous system needs time to process each new sensation. That's not slow—that's smart.

Research shows children using the SOS Approach see measurable improvements in food acceptance within weeks to months—not years of battles.

### CARD VIEW

HEADLINE (Cyan, Bold, 32px):
"Bridge Foods:
The Secret Weapon"

SUBTEXT (Gray, 14px):
"Meet them where they are. Expand from there."

[Learn More ℹ️]
```

### MODAL
```
TITLE: "How Bridge Foods Work"

BODY TEXT:
Here's where the magic happens.

Your child has foods they already like and eat comfortably. Chicken nuggets. Crackers. Pasta. Whatever they trust.

A "bridge food" shares the same sensory qualities as those safe foods—but introduces something slightly new.

Real Example:
→ If they like crunchy, salty crackers
→ Try crunchy, salty pretzels (similar texture, new taste)
→ Then gradually explore other crunchy snacks

It's not random. It's strategic. You're meeting them where they are, then slowly expanding from there.

How This App Helps:
We analyze your child's favorite foods, identify what they like about them (textures, flavors, temperatures), and suggest bridge foods they're most likely to accept.

This is how selective eaters become adventurous eaters. One bridge at a time.

## Implementation Notes

### UX Flow:
1. Card loads with headline + image + subtext
2. User can either:
   - Tap [Learn More ℹ️] to see full explanation in modal
   - Tap [Next →] to skip learning & proceed
3. Modal has [Next →] button to close & proceed

### Benefits:
✅ Cards stay clean & scannable
✅ Full explanation available if needed
✅ Parents can move quickly or learn deeply
✅ No cognitive overload
✅ Respects different learning styles

### Design Notes:
- Modals should be full-screen (mobile-first)
- Dark theme with cyan/gold accents
- Smooth animations (fade in/out)
- Close button on modal (X or back arrow)
- Body text should be readable (16px, line-height 1.8)
