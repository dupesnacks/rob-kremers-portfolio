## Three Business Models for Your App

### Model 1: Affiliate Commission (Fastest to Launch)

**How it works:**
- Partner with GLO Science, Zoom, Vivera, etc. as an affiliate
- When patient buys through your app, you get a commission (5-15%)
- Patient pays full retail
- You pocket the commission

**Pros:**
- Zero inventory, zero risk
- Easy to launch (just get affiliate links)
- Suppliers already have affiliate programs
- Fast cash flow

**Cons:**
- Lowest margin (5-10% is typical)
- You're not the trusted middleman (supplier still owns the relationship)
- Can't offer special pricing (patients know they're being marked up)
- Suppliers can change terms anytime

**Revenue Example (TFD):**
- 50 whitening kits/month × $150 × 7% commission = $525/month = $6,300/year
- Not life-changing but easy money

**How to set up:**
1. Contact GLO Science, Philips, Align directly
2. Ask for "dental practice affiliate program"
3. Get tracking links
4. Embed in app
5. They track conversions, you get paid monthly

---

### Model 2: Wholesale Partnership (Better Margins)

**How it works:**
- Buy retainers/whitening kits from supplier at wholesale (40-50% off retail)
- Sell through your app at retail (or slight discount)
- You pocket the difference

**Pros:**
- 40-50% gross margin (vs. 7% affiliate)
- You control pricing, branding, patient relationship
- Can offer loyalty/bulk discounts
- Build customer loyalty to your platform
- Sustainable business model

**Cons:**
- Requires capital (inventory)
- Need to handle logistics (order, storage, shipping)
- Supplier relationships need negotiation
- Returns/defects you handle

**Revenue Example (TFD):**
- 50 whitening kits/month: Buy at $75 wholesale, sell at $150
  - 50 × ($150-75) = $3,750/month = $45,000/year gross margin
  - After shipping/handling: ~$35K net

**How to set up:**
1. Contact Henry Schein, Patterson Dental, or direct suppliers (GLO, Philips)
2. Ask for "wholesale dental practice program"
3. Negotiate minimum order quantities (usually $500-2,000/month)
4. Set up account with payment/shipping terms
5. Buy stock, list in app, handle fulfillment

**Pro tip:** Start with ONE category (whitening kits) to test, then expand to retainers.

---

### Model 3: White-Label or Co-Branded (Best Positioning)

**How it works:**
- Partner with a manufacturer (e.g., GLO Science, ClearCorrect)
- App becomes the exclusive distribution channel for their products
- You potentially get the supplier's endorsement
- Custom packaging/branding with practice name
- Revenue share: You keep 30-40% of each sale

**Pros:**
- Strongest competitive moat (exclusive partnerships)
- High margins (30-40%)
- Supplier does marketing/credibility for you
- Custom-branded products = practice loyalty
- Positions you as THE platform for that supplier

**Cons:**
- Need to negotiate directly with suppliers (harder)
- Requires scale (suppliers want volume guarantees)
- More locked-in relationships (longer contracts)
- Need to handle exclusivity carefully

**Revenue Example (TFD at scale: 100 practices):**
- Partner with GLO Science for whitening
- Each practice sells ~100 kits/month
- 100 practices × 100 kits × $150 × 30% margin = $450K/month = $5.4M/year
- Platform takes 30%, passes 70% to practices

**How to set up:**
1. Build proof of concept with 1-2 practices (Model 2)
2. Show metrics to supplier ("This is how many kits we're moving")
3. Pitch: "I have 100+ practices. Want to be their exclusive whitening provider?"
4. Negotiate white-label/exclusivity deal
5. Roll out co-branded products to network

**Key supplier you should talk to first:**
- **GLO Science** (they actively support dental practices, have proven affiliate + wholesale programs)
- **Philips Zoom** (biggest name in professional whitening, established with dentists)

## Key Takeaway

**You don't need to own manufacturing.** You need to own the distribution and patient relationship.

The supplier (GLO, Philips, Align) makes the product. You own the practice + patient relationship. You become the trusted middleman. You take a cut, they move volume, practices get convenience.

**Execution order:**
1. Launch affiliate (this week)
2. Build metrics (next 3 months)
3. Approach one supplier with data (month 4)
4. Negotiate wholesale deal (month 5-6)
5. Scale white-label partnerships (month 8+)

By Year 2, consumables revenue becomes 20-30% of your app business. That's your moat. That's why practices stick with you instead of switching.
